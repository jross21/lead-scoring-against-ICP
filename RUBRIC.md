# ICP Scoring Rubric: HR Benefits & Wellness Perks Platform

**Product summary:** Helps HR and People Ops leaders deliver flexible wellness stipends, curated perks, and benefit bundles through a single employee-facing marketplace.

**Target geography:** US-based only
**Employee range:** 100–2,500 employees
**Sweet spot:** 150–800 employees, multi-state or remote-first workforce

---

## Tier 1: Ideal Customer

A lead qualifies as Tier 1 if ALL of the following are true:

- **US-headquartered.** Primary operations in the United States. Verify via LinkedIn HQ or website footer. Multi-state or remote-first workforces are especially strong signals.
- **Employee count between 150–800 (sweet spot).** Accept 100–2,500 for Tier 1 if all other criteria are met. Fewer than 100 lacks HR infrastructure; over 2,500 likely has deeply embedded enterprise HR systems.
- **Identifiable People Ops or HR function.** At least one VP of People, HR Director, or Benefits Manager on LinkedIn or in job postings. Headless HR (founder-only) is a Tier 2 signal.
- **Multi-state or remote-first workforce.** Distributed teams create the core pain point: inconsistent benefits, vendor fragmentation, and compliance complexity. Confirm via careers page or LinkedIn employee locations.
- **Series A or later, OR profitable/self-sustaining.** Must have sufficient budget for discretionary HR software. Pre-seed and seed-stage companies typically cannot justify the spend.
- **In a target vertical.** SaaS/Tech, Professional Services, Healthcare Admin or Healthtech, Financial Services, Marketing Agencies, or Consulting — verticals where talent competition and retention are core business concerns.

---

## Tier 2: Workable

A lead qualifies as Tier 2 if it meets some but not all Tier 1 criteria:

- **Smaller (50–100) or larger (800–2,500) employee count.** Smaller companies may have a founder or office manager handling HR. Still workable if there's evidence of a benefits conversation or rapid hiring. Larger accounts may face more procurement friction.
- **Adjacent industries with talent-competitive environments.** B2B services, media, e-commerce, logistics-tech, or education-tech where employee retention is a priority. Confirm the company cares about culture via Glassdoor or careers page.
- **HR function exists but is early-stage.** Single HR generalist or HR Manager (not yet a VP or Director). Strong buying signal if they recently posted a senior People Ops hire.
- **Primarily in-office but expanding to hybrid or remote.** Transition toward distributed work creates the same pain points as fully remote. Look for recent policy changes in press or job descriptions.

---

## Disqualifiers (DQ)

DQ if ANY of the following are true:

- Fewer than 50 employees — insufficient HR infrastructure and budget maturity.
- Over 2,500 employees — enterprise HR platforms (Workday, SAP) are deeply embedded; displacement is very difficult.
- B2C, consumer product, or marketplace — no HR/People Ops buying committee; product does not map to the use case.
- Government, defense, or heavily regulated public sector — benefits are often union-negotiated or legislatively mandated.
- Outside the United States — legal, tax, and compliance requirements for employee benefits are not currently supported in non-US markets.
- Personal email domain (gmail, yahoo, etc.) — not a real business buyer; do not route into pipeline.

---

## Buying Signals

Buying signals do not change the tier — they raise confidence within it. A Tier 2 company with 4 buying signals may deserve higher-priority outreach but still routes into the Tier 2 sequence.

| Signal | What to look for |
|---|---|
| Recently raised funding | New capital often unlocks HR and benefits spend. Series A/B within last 12 months is especially strong. |
| Aggressive hiring | 10+ open roles across functions signals headcount growth and talent competition pressure. |
| Glassdoor or Blind pressure | Sub-4.0 Glassdoor rating or reviews citing poor benefits signals urgent pain and buyer motivation. |
| "Employee wellness" on careers page | Explicit wellness language indicates a value-level commitment, not just a nice-to-have. |
| Recently hired HR leader | New VP of People or HR Director often has a mandate to modernize benefits. High receptivity to new vendors. |
| Fragmented wellness vendors | Uses multiple point solutions (gym subsidy, Headspace, etc.) — strong consolidation opportunity. |
| Already offering stipends | Existing stipend program signals budget exists and willingness to invest; opportunity to consolidate and upgrade. |
| High Glassdoor pressure | Negative reviews citing benefits or culture create urgency for HR leadership to act. |

## Economic Buyer by Company Size

| Company size | Primary buyer | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 50–100 employees | Founder / CEO | No dedicated HR function yet. Benefits is a founder decision. |
| 100–1,000 employees | VP of People / HR Director / Chief People Officer | Also engage: Benefits Manager, People Ops Manager, CFO for budget approval. |
| 1,000–2,500 employees | CHRO / Finance / Procurement | Multi-stakeholder buy. Expect longer cycle and RFP process. CFO or Finance typically owns final sign-off. |

**Key titles to target:** VP of People, Chief People Officer, Director of Benefits, HR Operations Manager, People & Culture Manager

## Adjacent Tech Stack Signals

Use of these tools in job postings or the tech stack suggests an existing HR infrastructure that the platform integrates with or displaces:

BambooHR · Rippling · Workday · ADP Workforce Now · Gusto · Namely · Zenefits · Lattice · Slack · Microsoft Teams

---

## Scoring Notes

- When data is missing, prefer Tier 2 over Tier 1. The cost of a false positive (a Tier 1 that turns out to be a poor fit) is higher than a false negative.
- Set confidence to **low** when 2 or more of the following are unknown: employee count, funding stage, HR function, workforce distribution, or vertical.
- Always cite the specific evidence (LinkedIn, job posting, Glassdoor, Crunchbase) that drove the tier decision in every scored record.
- Buying signals raise confidence within a tier — they do not change the tier assignment.
